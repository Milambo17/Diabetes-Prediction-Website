DIABTES MELLITUS

Diabetes is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by high blood sugar levels. It occurs when the body either does not produce enough insulin or does not effectively use the insulin it produces. People having diabetes have high risk of diseases like heart disease, kidney disease, stroke, eye problem, nerve damage, etc.

According to the International Diabetes Federation (IDF), as of 2021, approximately 463 million adults (20-79 years) were living with diabetes worldwide. Diabetes prevalence is increasing globally, and it is estimated that by 2045, the number of people with diabetes will rise to 700 million.

Type 2 diabetes accounts for around 90% of all diabetes cases. Uncontrolled diabetes can have a significant impact on quality of life and life expectancy. Lifestyle factors such as unhealthy diet, physical inactivity, and obesity contribute to the increasing prevalence of Type 2 diabetes. Early diagnosis, effective management, and adopting a healthy lifestyle can help prevent or delay the onset of Type 2 diabetes.

Type 1 Diabetes

This type of diabetes is an autoimmune condition where the immune system mistakenly attacks and destroys insulin-producing cells in the pancreas. It usually develops in childhood or early adulthood. People with Type 1 diabetes require insulin injections or the use of an insulin pump to manage their blood sugar levels.

Type 2 Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes is the most common form of diabetes, accounting for the majority of cases. It is often associated with lifestyle factors such as obesity, sedentary behavior, and poor diet. In Type 2 diabetes, the body becomes resistant to insulin or does not produce enough insulin. It can be managed with lifestyle changes, oral medications, and in some cases, insulin therapy.

Gestational Diabetes

Gestational diabetes occurs during pregnancy and usually resolves after childbirth. It is characterized by high blood sugar levels that develop during pregnancy in women who did not have diabetes before. Gestational diabetes increases the risk of complications during pregnancy and delivery, and it may also increase the risk of Type 2 diabetes in both the mother and child.
